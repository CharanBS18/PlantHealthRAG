Tomato Early Blight:
Crop: Tomato
Stage: Vegetative to Fruiting
Region: Warm humid regions
Cause: Fungus Alternaria solani.
Symptoms: Brown spots with ring pattern on older leaves, yellow halo around spots, leaves dry and fall.
Treatment: Remove infected leaves, spray recommended fungicide, avoid wet leaves.
Prevention: Keep plant spacing, water at soil level, clean crop debris after harvest.

Tomato Late Blight:
Crop: Tomato
Stage: Vegetative to Fruiting
Region: Cool and wet regions
Cause: Phytophthora infestans.
Symptoms: Dark wet-looking patches on leaves, white growth under leaf, fast spread in rainy weather.
Treatment: Remove badly infected plants, spray copper-based fungicide early, reduce leaf wetness.
Prevention: Use resistant varieties, avoid overhead watering, improve field drainage.

Tomato Leaf Curl Virus:
Crop: Tomato
Stage: Early to Mid growth
Region: Tropical and subtropical
Cause: Virus spread by whiteflies.
Symptoms: Upward leaf curl, small leaves, stunted growth, poor fruit set.
Treatment: Remove heavily affected plants, control whiteflies, use yellow sticky traps.
Prevention: Use healthy seedlings, install insect net where possible, keep weeds low.

Tomato Septoria Leaf Spot:
Crop: Tomato
Stage: Mid to late season
Region: Humid regions
Cause: Fungus Septoria lycopersici.
Symptoms: Many small round spots with dark border and pale center on lower leaves.
Treatment: Remove infected leaves, use approved fungicide, avoid splashing water.
Prevention: Mulch soil, rotate crops, keep leaves dry.

Rice Blast:
Crop: Rice
Stage: Nursery to grain filling
Region: High humidity and low night temperature
Cause: Fungus Magnaporthe oryzae.
Symptoms: Spindle-shaped lesions with gray center on leaves, neck rot near panicle.
Treatment: Use recommended fungicide at early stage, balanced fertilizer, avoid excess nitrogen.
Prevention: Resistant varieties, proper spacing, avoid late heavy nitrogen dose.

Rice Bacterial Leaf Blight:
Crop: Rice
Stage: Tillering to maturity
Region: Warm wet regions
Cause: Bacteria Xanthomonas oryzae.
Symptoms: Yellow to white leaf streaks from tip, leaf drying, reduced tillers.
Treatment: Drain excess water, use recommended bactericide if advised locally, remove badly affected clumps.
Prevention: Use clean seed, avoid plant injury, balanced nutrients.

Chili Leaf Curl:
Crop: Chili
Stage: Early to flowering
Region: Warm regions
Cause: Virus spread by whiteflies.
Symptoms: Leaves curl upward, leaves become thick and small, plant remains short.
Treatment: Remove highly infected plants, control whiteflies, use sticky traps.
Prevention: Healthy seedlings, insect-proof nursery, weed control around field.

Powdery Mildew:
Crop: Many crops
Stage: Any stage
Region: Dry days and cool nights
Cause: Fungal infection.
Symptoms: White powder-like layer on leaf surface, yellowing, weak growth.
Treatment: Remove affected leaves, spray sulfur-based fungicide if suitable for crop.
Prevention: Good airflow, avoid overcrowding, monitor regularly.

Downy Mildew:
Crop: Many crops
Stage: Any stage
Region: Cool humid conditions
Cause: Oomycete infection.
Symptoms: Yellow patches on upper leaf, gray-purple growth under leaf.
Treatment: Remove infected leaves, spray recommended fungicide early.
Prevention: Reduce leaf wetness, morning irrigation only, clean tools.

Nitrogen Deficiency:
Crop: Many crops
Stage: Fast vegetative growth
Region: Any region
Cause: Low available nitrogen in soil.
Symptoms: Older leaves turn light green to yellow first, slow growth.
Treatment: Apply nitrogen-rich fertilizer in split doses, add compost.
Prevention: Soil test, balanced fertilizer schedule, maintain organic matter.

Potassium Deficiency:
Crop: Many crops
Stage: Flowering to fruiting
Region: Sandy or nutrient-poor soils
Cause: Low potassium availability.
Symptoms: Brown leaf margins, weak stems, poor fruit quality.
Treatment: Add potash fertilizer, irrigate after application.
Prevention: Soil testing, balanced NPK application, add crop residue compost.

Phosphorus Deficiency:
Crop: Many crops
Stage: Early growth
Region: Cold or compact soils
Cause: Poor phosphorus uptake.
Symptoms: Slow growth, dark green or purple leaves, weak roots.
Treatment: Apply phosphorus fertilizer near root zone.
Prevention: Correct soil pH, place fertilizer properly, improve root health.

Zinc Deficiency:
Crop: Rice, maize, citrus and others
Stage: Early growth
Region: High pH soils
Cause: Low zinc in soil.
Symptoms: Yellowing between veins on young leaves, short internodes.
Treatment: Apply zinc sulfate as soil or foliar spray as per label.
Prevention: Soil test, micronutrient mix in schedule, avoid over-liming.

Mango Anthracnose (Fruit Rot):
Crop: Mango
Stage: Fruiting and post-harvest
Region: Humid and rainy regions
Cause: Fungus Colletotrichum gloeosporioides.
Symptoms: Black sunken spots on fruit skin, dark patches expand during storage, cut fruit may show soft brown rot.
Treatment: Remove infected fruits, use recommended fungicide before harvest, handle fruits gently after harvest.
Prevention: Prune canopy for airflow, avoid fruit injury, keep orchard clean and dry.

Mango Stem-End Rot:
Crop: Mango
Stage: Post-harvest
Region: Warm humid regions
Cause: Fungal infection entering through stem end.
Symptoms: Black soft rot starts near stem end, internal fruit tissue turns brown after cutting.
Treatment: Harvest carefully with short stem, sort and remove affected fruits, use approved post-harvest treatment.
Prevention: Sanitize crates, avoid rough handling, store in cool dry place.

Banana Anthracnose:
Crop: Banana
Stage: Fruit ripening and storage
Region: Warm humid regions
Cause: Fungal infection.
Symptoms: Black circular sunken spots on peel, fruit flesh may soften under spots.
Treatment: Remove affected fruits, keep fruits dry, use recommended fungicide program in field.
Prevention: Bag bunches where practiced, avoid fruit injuries, keep packing area clean.

Citrus Canker:
Crop: Citrus
Stage: Leaf flush to fruiting
Region: Warm wet regions
Cause: Bacterial infection.
Symptoms: Raised corky lesions on leaves and fruit, yellow halo around lesions, fruit quality drops.
Treatment: Remove badly affected twigs, spray copper-based product as advised locally, control leaf miner.
Prevention: Use disease-free nursery plants, sanitize tools, avoid movement of infected material.

Citrus Greening (HLB):
Crop: Citrus
Stage: Any stage
Region: Tropical and subtropical
Cause: Bacteria spread by psyllid insect.
Symptoms: Uneven yellow leaves, small lopsided fruits, bitter taste, poor yield.
Treatment: Remove severely affected trees, manage psyllid population, improve nutrition and irrigation.
Prevention: Use certified healthy seedlings, monitor psyllids regularly, remove nearby infected hosts.

Apple Scab:
Crop: Apple
Stage: Leaf emergence to fruiting
Region: Cool moist regions
Cause: Fungus Venturia inaequalis.
Symptoms: Olive-brown velvety spots on leaves and fruits, fruit cracks in severe cases.
Treatment: Remove infected leaves/fruits, spray recommended fungicide schedule.
Prevention: Orchard sanitation, pruning for airflow, avoid long leaf wetness period.

Guava Fruit Fly Damage:
Crop: Guava
Stage: Fruiting
Region: Warm regions
Cause: Fruit fly infestation.
Symptoms: Small puncture marks on fruit, internal maggots, fruit rots after cutting.
Treatment: Collect and destroy infested fruits, use protein bait traps, bag fruits.
Prevention: Orchard hygiene, regular trap monitoring, timely harvest.

Pomegranate Bacterial Blight:
Crop: Pomegranate
Stage: Flowering to fruiting
Region: Warm humid regions
Cause: Bacterial infection.
Symptoms: Black spots on leaves/fruits, oily water-soaked lesions, fruit cracking.
Treatment: Remove affected branches/fruits, spray advised bactericide schedule, avoid overhead irrigation.
Prevention: Clean pruning tools, proper spacing, balanced nutrients.

Coconut Bud Rot:
Crop: Coconut
Stage: Seedling to young palm
Region: High rainfall regions
Cause: Fungal-like infection.
Symptoms: Central spindle leaf rots, foul smell, crown may collapse.
Treatment: Remove rotted tissue carefully, apply recommended protective paste/fungicide.
Prevention: Improve drainage, avoid water stagnation, monitor crown regularly.

Tree Bark Canker:
Crop: Fruit trees and timber trees
Stage: Any stage
Region: Many regions
Cause: Fungal or bacterial infection through wounds.
Symptoms: Sunken cracked bark, gum oozing, branch dieback.
Treatment: Prune infected branches, clean wound area, apply recommended wound protectant.
Prevention: Disinfect pruning tools, avoid trunk injury, improve tree vigor.
